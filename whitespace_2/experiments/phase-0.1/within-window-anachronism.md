## Decision support

**For 'hard' anachronism concepts (term coined post-2000, no precursor):**
any score≥0.3 pre-1990 paper is a likely anachronism. If hard-category
concepts have meaningful pre-1990 score≥0.3 counts, the OpenAlex classifier
is producing genuine anachronism within ws2's window. Counts ≤2 per concept
are likely tolerable noise; counts ≥10 indicate systematic anachronism.

**For 'medium' / 'soft' concepts:** pre-1990 score≥0.3 papers may be genuine
precursors (e.g., 1980s neural-net papers tagged 'Deep learning' at low
score). Spot-check titles to disambiguate.

## Final assessment (after title inspection)

**Within-window anachronism is REAL** for ~6 of 10 testable hard-category
concepts, with two distinct mechanisms:

### Mechanism 1: Keyword-matching false positives

The classifier latches on isolated keyword matches without semantic context.
Examples from the spot-checks:

- **Big data** 1974: "Movement of Drugs Across the Gills of Fishes" (score=0.75).
  About fish pharmacokinetics. Likely matched on "data" appearing many times.
  35/50 pre-1990 score≥0.5. Catastrophic anachronism.
- **Cloud computing** 1976: "A Short Course in Cloud Physics" (score=0.76),
  1988: "AVHRR cloudy radiances" (score=0.72). Atmospheric clouds, not cloud
  computing. Keyword "cloud" matched.
- **World Wide Web** 1977: "Steric effects of phosphorus ligands in
  organometallic chemistry" (score=0.41). Keyword "web" or related matched.
  But all WWW pre-1990 score≥0.3 papers stay below score=0.5, so the
  classifier is at least being cautious.
- **Word embedding** 1975: "Embedding Algebras with Solvable Word Problems"
  (score=0.56). Pure mathematics. Keywords "embedding" + "word" matched in
  unrelated context.

### Mechanism 2: Junk `publication_year` metadata

Some papers in OpenAlex have `publication_year` values that are clearly wrong
— typically 1970, suggesting a sentinel/default value when the real year is
unavailable. Examples:

- **Augmented reality** 1970: "Brave new world: Mobile phones, museums and
  learning - how and why to use Augmented Reality" (score=0.89). The title is
  obviously about a post-2010 paper; year=1970 is metadata garbage.
- **CNN** 1970: "BRAIN TUMOR DETECTION USING FASTER REGION-BASED
  CONVOLUTIONAL NEURAL NETWORKS ON CT AND MR" (score=0.79). R-CNN is a 2015
  method; this is metadata error.
- **IoT** 1970: "Power Condition Maintenance Based on Internet of Things
  Technology" (score=0.61). IoT didn't exist in 1970.

The 1970 sentinel-year problem is real and propagates anachronism into ws2's
analytical window even when the classifier itself is correct (it's tagging
the paper based on its actual content; the dirty year metadata makes the
paper appear in a wrong era's analytical bucket).

### Soft-category concepts: mostly LEGITIMATE pre-1990 tagging

- **Recurrent neural network**: 13/50 score≥0.3 includes Hochreiter-Schmidhuber-
  era classics: "Learning State Space Trajectories in Recurrent Neural
  Networks" (1989, cited=674), "Generalization of back-propagation to RNN"
  (1987, cited=952). These are genuine precursors, correctly tagged.
- **Machine learning**: "Induction of decision trees" (1986, cited=12,350),
  "Genetic algorithms" (1989, cited=49,332). Genuine ML classics.
- **Artificial intelligence**: "Snakes: Active contour models" (1988),
  "Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems" (Pearl 1988). Genuine AI.
- **Neural network**: "Theory of the backpropagation neural network" (1989,
  cited=2,233). Genuine. 17/50 score≥0.5 are mostly real 1980s NN papers.

So soft-category concepts (terms older than 1990) tag genuinely on 1970-89
papers. The "pre-1990 papers tagged with these" is mostly correct.

## What this means for ws2

### Decision-relevant findings

1. **Within-window anachronism IS real, but confined to specific concept
   types.** Specifically: hard-category concepts (terms post-2000) are
   contaminated by:
   - Keyword-match false positives (Big data, Cloud computing, etc.)
   - Junk publication_year metadata (Augmented reality, CNN, IoT)

2. **The two mechanisms have different mitigation paths:**
   - Keyword-match anachronism: avoided entirely by using embedding-cluster
     subfields instead of concept tags. The §11 cluster-fit commitment
     regains force — for this specific reason, not the original (now-
     retracted) "promiscuous tagger" reason.
   - Junk year metadata: requires a separate cleanliness filter. Papers with
     `publication_year < 1990` whose titles mention obviously post-2000
     concepts (R-CNN, IoT, mobile phones, etc.) should be flagged or
     excluded. This is a Stage-1 data-cleaning concern.

3. **Soft-category concepts are FINE.** ws2 can use "Neural network",
   "Machine learning", "Artificial intelligence" etc. as subfield labels
   without major within-window anachronism concerns. Modern technical-
   neologism concepts (Big data, Cloud computing, IoT) cannot.

### Phase 0.2 commitments (refined)

1. **§11 cluster-fit-on-temporally-stratified-pooled-subsample** stays as
   the *preferred* subfield mechanism. Reason: avoids the keyword-match
   anachronism for hard-category concepts. (Not because OpenAlex concepts
   are broadly broken — see Check 2 correction.)

2. **OpenAlex concept tags as auxiliary features** — viable for
   soft-category concepts at score≥0.3, NOT viable for hard-category
   concepts on pre-1990 papers. List of concept-tag-safe-pre-1990 vs
   concept-tag-unsafe-pre-1990 maintained as a Methods-section table.

3. **New Stage-1 commitment — junk-year-metadata filter.** Papers with
   `publication_year < 1990` whose abstract/title contains tokens
   indicating post-2000 origin (e.g., "R-CNN", "IoT", "mobile phone",
   "blockchain", etc.) get a `suspect_year` flag. Flagged papers are
   excluded from era-stratified analyses unless year is independently
   verified.

4. **Score-threshold pre-registration** (already locked in Check 2
   correction): all `concepts.id:X` filters use score≥0.3 (loose) or
   ≥0.5 (strict) thresholds. Pre-register specific thresholds per use
   case in Phase 0.2.

### Limitations language

ws2's analytical population is "OpenAlex CS+Physics 1970-2024 papers with
non-empty abstract_inverted_index, plus a junk-year-metadata cleanliness
filter." Subfield assignment is via embedding-clusters (§11) with
score-thresholded concept tags as auxiliary features for soft-category
concepts only.
